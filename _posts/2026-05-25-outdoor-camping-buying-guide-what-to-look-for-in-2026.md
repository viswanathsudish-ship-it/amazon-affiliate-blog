## Buying Guide: How to Choose Camping Gear That Lasts

**Match gear to trip type first.** Backpacking and car camping require completely different equipment. A 4.5 lb backpack that's perfect for multi-day hikes is excessive for car camping where weight is irrelevant. Decide your trip type before buying anything.

**Weight matters more than you think.** Every pound in a backpack translates to measurable fatigue over a long day. For backpacking, target a base weight (pack + gear without food and water) under 20 lbs. For car camping, weight is irrelevant.

**Capacity for coolers and packs.** A 65L backpack carries 4–6 days of gear for one person. A 45-quart cooler holds approximately 28 cans with ice — adequate for 2 people for 3–4 days. Don't overbuy capacity if your trips are shorter.

**Weather rating for tents.** Three-season tents (like the Coleman Sundome) handle spring through fall in most conditions. Four-season tents handle snow loads and high winds. Most campers never need a four-season tent.
